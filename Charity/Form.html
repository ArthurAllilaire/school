What does Papyrus do?

 

Suicide is the single biggest killer of people under the age of 35 in the UK today. We are asking for your help in changing that.

 

Papyrus is the national suicide prevention charity. Their mission is to reduce the number of young people who take their own lives by shattering the stigma around suicide and equipping young people and their communities with the skills to recognise and respond to suicidal behaviour.

 

However, suicide is still an epidemic that is touching all corners of Britain, with 10 deaths per 100,000 people per year in the UK. Suicide is touching young people everywhere and in all walks of life.

Papyrus is changing this in three main ways:

 

Support:

 

Papyrus provides confidential support and advice to young people struggling with thoughts of suicide, and anyone worried about a young person through their helpline, HOPELINEUK.

 

Just £5 raised can fund one life-saving call, text or email to HOPELINEUK.

 

 

Equip:

 

Papyrus engages communities, the UK Government and volunteers in suicide prevention projects and deliver training programmes to individuals and groups. This includes equipping local councils, healthcare professionals and school staff with suicide prevention skills.

 

Influence:

 

Papyrus tackles the systemic causes of suicide as they are a member of many government advisory groups in the UK on suicide prevention, where they present evidence so that lessons can be learned and learning implemented to help save young lives.

Papyrus also use hard-hitting and dynamic social media campaigns to raise awareness of suicide and shatter the stigma around it.

 

To find out more please have a look at their website: https://www.papyrus-uk.org/who-we-are-2/.

 

We believe that this makes them one of the most effective charities at preventing suicide. They have experienced a 25% rise in contacts to their helpline, highlighting the severity and pressing nature of suicide.
